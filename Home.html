Transatlanticism

Death Cab for Cutie

Frontman Gibbard wrote a noteworthy but uneven collection of songs that detail the tumultuous emotions involved when it comes to long distance relationships. However, even though the second half of the album is bogged down by the unnecessary tracks Death of an Interior Decorator and A Lack of Color, its stellar tracks Tiny Vessels, Expo '86, and the iconic "Transatlanticism" cut twice as hard.

Saintmotelevision

Saint Motel

Saint Motel have always had a flair for theatrics. However, on their sophomore album, they've lost that touch of charm we've come to expect from them. Despite opening strong with songs like Move and Destroyer, the album meanders with sugary pop empowerment You Can Be You and utterly mediocre Local Long Distance Relationship (LA2NY. Perhaps if they could capture that magic that brought them to forefront with 2014's My Type, they could bounce back from their sophomore slump.
